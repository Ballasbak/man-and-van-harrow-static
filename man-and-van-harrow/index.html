What "Man & Van" actually means

You pay by the hour. The mover quotes an hourly rate covering the van and crew. The total depends on how long the job takes, including travel between addresses.

Best for smaller moves. Studios, 1-bed flats, single rooms, part-loads, single-item collections and short cross-town moves.

Crew size scales with the job. Most Harrow Man & Van jobs go out as 1 van + 2 crew. Larger loads use 1 van + 3 crew or 2 vans.

Local detail

Parking and access in Harrow. Most of central Harrow, Wealdstone and South Harrow has on-street parking with permit zones. Movers will normally load from the nearest legal spot rather than block the road. For flats above ground floor without a lift (common in Pinner and parts of North Harrow), expect a small carry surcharge or longer hourly time.

Common Harrow Man & Van jobs. Student and young-professional moves around Harrow on the Hill, single-room moves in HMOs around Wealdstone and West Harrow, end-of-tenancy moves out of 1-bed flats in Kenton and Rayners Lane, and small cross-borough moves to Wembley, Edgware and Ruislip.

Distance. Man & Van pricing assumes a local job. Moves much beyond the 80-mile mark are usually better priced as House Removals — see House Removals in Harrow.

Common questions

How is Man & Van priced?
By the hour. The mover quotes an hourly rate and the final price is rate × hours (including a small travel allowance).
Is there a minimum charge?
Most Harrow movers have a 2-hour minimum.
What size moves does Man & Van cover?
Single items up to roughly a 2-bed flat. Bigger than that, House Removals is usually cheaper because it's priced as a whole job.
Do I have to help carry?
No. Crew sizes are quoted based on the load. You can add helpers if you want to speed things up.
How quickly can someone come?
Same-day and next-day slots are common in Harrow.
